The Direct Costs (The Tip of the Iceberg)

These are the hard costs that appear on your P&L statement. While painful, they are easy to quantify:

💸

Sunk Partner Fees

The money already paid to the original partner for an incomplete, broken, or dysfunctional system. For a mid-sized Odoo implementation, this is typically $30,000 to $150,000+.

📜

Idle License Subscriptions

Paying for Odoo Enterprise user seats and hosting (Odoo.sh or On-Premise servers) month after month for a system that isn't live or isn't being used. This can easily run $1,000–$5,000 per month.

🔧

Re-work & Rescue Fees

The cost of hiring a new firm (like AdataSol) to audit the damage, fix the broken code, and complete the implementation properly.

The Hidden Costs (The 90% Below the Surface)

The hidden costs are insidious. They don't appear as a single line item on a bill, but they drain your working capital and growth potential every single day the implementation remains broken.

⏳

Productivity Drain & Dual-Entry

When Odoo doesn't work properly, employees are forced to use workarounds—usually Excel. This means entering data twice (once in the legacy system/spreadsheet, once in Odoo), which doubles labor costs and guarantees data discrepancies. If 20 employees waste just 10 hours a month on workarounds at $30/hr, that's $72,000 a year in pure waste.

📉

Opportunity Cost (The ROI You Aren't Getting)

Why did you buy Odoo? To optimize inventory, close books faster, or improve sales tracking. Every month the system is broken, you are losing the financial benefit of those improvements. Delayed ROI often costs businesses hundreds of thousands of dollars.

🚪

Employee Turnover

Frustration with broken systems is a leading cause of burnout. When top performers leave because they are tired of fighting the ERP, you lose institutional knowledge and must pay recruiting and training costs (typically 50-200% of their salary).

📊

Decision Paralysis

If management cannot trust the reports coming out of Odoo, they are making strategic decisions blind. Bad data leads to bad investments, overstocking, or missed market opportunities.
