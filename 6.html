How to Choose an Odoo Rescue Partner (7 Critical Criteria)

Rescuing a failed ERP requires a completely different skill set than building one from scratch. Don't hire the wrong partner twice. Here is how to vet an Odoo rescue expert.

Talk to Our Rescue Team

Realizing your Odoo implementation is failing is hard. Deciding to hire a new partner to fix it is harder. But the hardest part? Finding a partner who actually has the specific skills required to rescue a broken project.

Most Odoo partners excel at "greenfield" implementations—starting from a blank database. But an Odoo implementation rescue is "brownfield" work. It requires reverse-engineering bad code, untangling corrupted data, and diplomatically managing a traumatized team. Before you sign another contract, use this guide to ensure you're hiring the right rescue partner.

The 7 Critical Criteria for an Odoo Rescue Partner

1. Specific Rescue & Recovery Experience

Ask the partner directly: "What percentage of your projects are rescues vs. new implementations?" If rescue is just a side service, they may lack the forensic skills needed. You want a team that actively specializes in Odoo implementation rescue and has a proven track record of stepping into failed projects.

2. Deep Technical Depth (Code & Database)

Rescue requires looking under the hood. Your partner must have senior Python/PostgreSQL developers who can read poorly written custom code, identify security vulnerabilities, and optimize slow database queries. If a partner only configures Odoo via the UI without understanding the backend architecture, they cannot rescue a deeply broken system. See how we rescue broken customizations →

3. A Proven Diagnostic Methodology

A good rescue partner won't just give you a quote based on a phone call. They should insist on an Odoo ERP Audit first. They must have a structured process for identifying the root causes of failure—whether it's data corruption, bad code, or process misalignment—before proposing a solution.

4. Transparent Communication & Diplomacy

Your team is likely frustrated, and pointing fingers. A great rescue partner acts as a neutral mediator. They must communicate what went wrong objectively (without unfairly bashing the previous partner) and provide clear, jargon-free updates on the recovery progress.

5. Reference Checks from Rescue Clients

Don't just ask for general references—ask to speak with clients whose projects they rescued. Ask the reference: "Did they stick to the rescue timeline? Was the data fully recovered? Did your team trust the system after they left?"

6. Flexible Engagement Models

Rescue projects have more unknowns than fresh implementations. A good rescue partner will offer flexible pricing—such as a fixed-price audit followed by agile sprint-based remediation—rather than forcing you into a massive, rigid contract upfront.

7. Post-Rescue Stabilization Support

Fixing the code isn't the end. The system needs to be stabilized, and your team needs to be re-trained. Ensure your partner offers comprehensive post-implementation stabilization to prevent relapse.

Red Flags: Warning Signs of the Wrong Rescue Partner

  • They promise a fixed price without auditing first. (They are guessing, which means you'll pay for their guess later.)
  • They tell you to scrap everything and start over immediately. Starting over is sometimes necessary, but it shouldn't be the default recommendation without evidence.
  • They lack in-house developers. If they outsource the coding, they can't control the quality or speed of the rescue.

15 Must-Ask Questions During Your Selection Process

Before you hire your rescue partner, ask them these 15 questions to verify their capability:

  1. How many Odoo rescue projects have you completed in the last 2 years?
  2. Can you share a case study where you recovered corrupted data migration?
  3. What is your process for auditing a failed implementation?
  4. Who will be the dedicated project manager for the rescue?
  5. Do your developers write automated tests for fixed code?
  6. How do you handle knowledge transfer from the previous partner?
  7. What happens if you discover the scope of the failure is worse than the audit revealed?
  8. Can you ensure we don't lose the working parts of our current system?
  9. What is your average time-to-stabilization for a rescue project?
  10. How do you manage change fatigue with our internal team?
  11. Do you offer post-rescue hypercare?
  12. Will we own the source code for any fixes you write?
  13. How do you ensure custom fixes won't break during the next Odoo version upgrade?
  14. What is your escalation path if a critical issue arises during rescue?
  15. Can we speak to a client whose project you rescued?
